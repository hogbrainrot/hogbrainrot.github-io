            <h2>Kingdom Hearts is...</h2>
                <h3>Lame!</h3>
                    I get it! How can a Final Fantasy action RPG with Donald Duck as a party member be any good? How can it have such a big, dedicated fanbase? I asked these questions in my sophomore year of high school, after watching a video of a Twitch streamer cursing out Mickey Mouse for trapping an anime girl in hell. The Kingdom Hearts games have their fans, but they also have a reputation for being overdramatic, lengthy, awkward messes. But, still, they had their fans. I had to learn more.
                <h3>Fun!</h3>
                    Upon starting the first Kingdom Hearts game from 2001, I fell in love. The combat was very simple, something that the later games change completely, but the magic system was surprisingly deep, and there were several secret uses of magic to be found within the game's worlds. The tone of this game was adorable-- The premise is, indeed, stupid, but the game fully understands this, strategically playing it straight when the stakes are high and leaning into the inherent silliness of it all when there's room for humor.  
                <h3>Beautiful!</h3>
                    The next game, Kingdom Hearts: Chain of Memories for the GBA, carried a lot of the same tone as the first game, but a certain weight lies underneath its cutesy demeanor. The main characters slowly lose their memories, puling the once-close group of friends further and further apart. There's a quiet sense of dread, watching these people (and Donald Duck and Goofy) slowly lose everything they've built over the course of the last game. By the end of the game, I felt uneasy. Going into the next one, I was blown away. Kingdom Hearts II is the most beautiful, grand, bittersweet game I've ever played. From the beginning, you can tell something's changed-- the main character from the last few games isn't even here! The boy who stands in his place is a troubled boy who wants nothing more than to enjoy his summer vacation with his friends, despite intereference from a mysterious force that he wants <strong>nothing</strong> to do with. I'd continue from here, but I can't give anything more away. Play Kingdom Hearts II! The title screen makes me cry!
